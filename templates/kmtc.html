Frequently Asked Questions About KMTC Programs

KMTC offers Diploma programs in Nursing, Clinical Medicine, Laboratory Technology, Radiography, Environmental Health, Physiotherapy, and Dental Technology. These are all 3-year programs designed to train healthcare professionals.

KMTC graduates are in high demand. Many get employed by the Ministry of Health, public hospitals, and private healthcare facilities. Some also pursue further specialization or private practice.

Biology and Chemistry are critically important for KMTC programs as they form the foundation of medical training. English language proficiency is also essential for communication and further studies.

KMTC programs are full-time with clinical placements in hospitals, making outside employment difficult. However, students may get some clinical experience allowances during their hospital rotations.

Starting salaries for KMTC graduates in government positions range from 30,000 to 50,000 Kenya shillings per month, with regular increases and benefits. Private sector positions may offer higher salaries.

KMTC Program Prerequisites and Requirements

Minimum Academic Requirements
  • Overall KCSE Grade: Minimum C+ (10 points) for most programs
  • Biology and Chemistry: Minimum C (5 points) each - critical subjects
  • English Language: Minimum C (5 points) for communication
  • Cluster Points: Varies by program, typically 40-60 points
  • Age Requirement: Preferably 18 years or older at entry
Program-Specific Requirements
  • All KMTC Programs: Excellent performance in Biology and Chemistry essential
  • Nursing/Midwifery: Compassion for patient care, good physical health
  • Laboratory Technology: Attention to detail, accuracy, analytical skills
  • Clinical Medicine: Strong scientific knowledge, clinical aptitude
  • Medical Screening: Health screening required before admission
