Higher Education Loans Board (HELB) - Government Loans

About HELB:

  • Loan Type: Government-subsidized loans, not scholarships (you repay)
  • Maximum Coverage: Up to KES 400,000 per year
  • Interest Rate: Lower than commercial banks - very affordable
  • Repayment Period: After graduation with grace period (typically 6 months)
  • Application: Apply during KUCCPS application process
  • Eligibility: Kenyan citizens admitted to accredited institutions

Major Scholarship Programs in Kenya

Well-Known Scholarship Opportunities:

  • HELB: Government loans available to all admitted students (apply during KUCCPS)
  • Kenya Vision 2030 Scholarship: For students in strategic sectors
  • County Bursaries: Each county offers bursaries - apply at county offices
  • Bank Scholarships: KCB, Equity, Standard Chartered often offer scholarships
  • Religious Organization Scholarships: Churches and religious groups fund students
  • Professional Body Scholarships: Law, engineering, medical bodies offer scholarships
  • International Scholarships: Search MasterCard Foundation, British Council, etc.

Combining Multiple Funding Sources

Financial Aid Strategy:

  • Layered Approach: Combine HELB + scholarship + work + family support
  • Part-Time Work: Work during holidays and weekends to fund additional needs
  • Merit Increases: Excel academically - some institutions increase scholarships
  • Check Stacking Rules: Some scholarships prohibit combining - read terms carefully
  • Budget Carefully: Plan for tuition, accommodation, books, and living expenses
  • Explore Work-Study: Many universities offer on-campus jobs with flexible hours

Frequently Asked Questions About Scholarships

Who is eligible for scholarships?

Eligibility varies widely. Most require: Kenyan citizenship, admission to an institution, reasonable grades, and demonstration of need (for need-based aid). Some require specific background or study field.

Can I apply for multiple scholarships?

Yes, you should apply for several! However, check terms - some scholarships prohibit "stacking" (receiving multiple simultaneously). Always disclose other funding to avoid breaching terms.

What if I don't get a full scholarship?

Most students combine funding: partial scholarship + HELB loan + part-time work + family support. This mixed approach is very common and manageable.

Where do I find scholarship opportunities?

Check: university websites, HELB website (www.helb.co.ke), county education offices, NGO websites, bank websites, professional bodies, and international scholarship databases.

When should I apply for HELB?

Apply during KUCCPS application process. HELB applications are integrated with KUCCPS placement. If you get placed, HELB can disburse loans directly to your institution.
