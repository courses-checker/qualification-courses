Frequently Asked Questions About Artisan Programs

Popular artisan trades include plumbing, electrical installation, carpentry, welding, motor mechanics, masonry, hairdressing, catering, and beauty therapy. Each provides specific technical skills for job placement or self-employment.

Absolutely. Artisan training is specifically designed for self-employment. Many graduates start their own workshops, businesses, or work as independent contractors with excellent earning potential.

Artisan programs have flexible entry requirements, typically minimum D- or D grade in KCSE. The focus is on practical aptitude and willingness to learn rather than high academic grades.

Most artisan programs take 1-2 years. Some trades may offer accelerated programs or apprenticeships. The duration varies by trade and institution offering the training.

You will earn an Artisan Certificate or Trade Certificate recognized by relevant trade bodies and employers. These qualifications allow you to practice your trade, register as a tradesperson, and operate your own business.

Artisan Program Prerequisites and Requirements

Minimum Academic Requirements
  • Overall KCSE Grade: Minimum D- or D grade - flexible requirements
  • Form 4 Completion: Basic secondary school education
  • Entry Score: Very flexible, low cluster point requirements
  • Age Requirement: Usually 16+ years at entry
  • Additional: Basic literacy and numeracy skills helpful
Program-Specific Requirements
  • All Trades: Physical fitness and ability to perform manual work
  • Electrical/Plumbing: Safety awareness, attention to detail important
  • Mechanical Trades: Problem-solving ability and mechanical aptitude
  • Beauty/Hairdressing: Interpersonal skills, creativity, patience
  • Health Requirements: Good health and ability to work in trade environments
